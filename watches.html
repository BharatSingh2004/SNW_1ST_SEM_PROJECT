Watch Restoration Guide

Keep your timepieces ticking and shining

Why Restore Watches?

Restoring watches not only maintains their functionality but also preserves their aesthetic appeal and value, especially for vintage or heirloom pieces.

Tips for Watch Restoration

  • Clean the case and strap: Use a soft cloth and mild soap for metal or rubber straps; use leather cleaner for leather straps.
  • Inspect the movement: Check for issues like slow ticking or irregular movement.
  • Replace worn-out parts: Change batteries, worn gaskets, or damaged straps as needed.
  • Polish scratches: Use a polishing cloth or paste for minor scratches on metal surfaces.
  • Test water resistance: Ensure proper seals to maintain waterproof capabilities.

Tricks for Watch Care

Ensure your watches remain in excellent condition with these tricks:

  • Store watches in a watch box or winder to prevent dust and moisture exposure.
  • Regularly wind mechanical watches to keep the movement in good condition.
  • Avoid magnetic fields to prevent interference with the watch's accuracy.
  • Service your watch every 3-5 years to keep it functioning perfectly.
